How to vote
Your first vote — step by step
Step 1
Get EMBER
Deposit stXRP → stake cXRP in CinderMining → earn EMBER over time. Or buy in the public sale.
Step 2 — Critical
Self-delegate
EMBER doesn't vote automatically. You must call delegate(yourAddress) once to activate your votes. Use the delegation box below.
Step 3
Find a proposal
Wait for a proposal you care about. Voting opens 2 days after creation. Your vote weight is your EMBER balance at the block the proposal was created — not when you vote.
Step 4
Cast your vote
Click a proposal → choose For / Against / Abstain → optionally add a reason → sign the transaction. Costs a tiny amount of FLR for gas. Your vote is permanent and on-chain.
⚠ The delegation step is the one people miss. Without it your EMBER has zero voting weight even if you have millions of tokens.

FAQ

All your EMBER questions.

Three ways: (1) Liquidity mining — stake your cXRP in CinderMining.sol and earn EMBER every second, proportional to your share of total staked cXRP. (2) Public sale — participate when announced through official channels. (3) Community airdrop — if you used Cinder or Firelight early, you may qualify. There is no private sale or VC allocation. The founder holds 10% with a 2-year linear vest (no cliff — earns daily).
EMBER governance controls the treasury, which accumulates 10% of all stXRP yield + 0.1% of every withdrawal. As Cinder TVL grows, so does treasury income. EMBER holders vote on how that value is deployed: buybacks, stXRP distributions, liquidity seeding, or grants. At $10M TVL and 9.3% gross APY, the treasury earns roughly $90,000/year in stXRP — and that compounds as TVL grows.
No lock-up required. Your voting power is your EMBER balance (or whoever you've delegated to). You do need to self-delegate first — this activates checkpointing. Once delegated, your votes are live for any proposal created after that block. You can transfer, sell, or re-delegate at any time with no penalty or waiting period.
Three layers: (1) Quorum — 40M EMBER (4% of supply) must vote For; a small minority can't quietly pass changes. (2) Timelock — 2-day mandatory delay after passing; depositors can exit if they disagree. (3) Guardian veto — a multisig guardian can cancel malicious proposals but has no other power (no execution, no funds access). Critically, vault withdrawals can never be blocked by governance — depositors always get their stXRP back.
CinderMining v2 emits EMBER every second across two pools: cFLR (60% of emission) and cXRP (40%). Each pool splits its allocation pro-rata across all staked shares in that pool. If you hold 10% of staked cXRP during Year 1, you earn 10% × 108M (60% of 180M) = 10.8M EMBER over the year. APR = (your annual EMBER × EMBER price) ÷ (your staked cXRP × stXRP price). The APR estimator above lets you model different scenarios. APR naturally decreases as more people stake (your share shrinks) and increases as EMBER price rises.
No. Total supply is fixed at deployment — there is no admin mint function. The allocation is hardcoded in the constructor and cannot be modified. The only mutable parameters (fee rates, quorum threshold, voting periods) can be changed solely by a passing EMBER governance vote, which requires 4% quorum and a 2-day timelock.
